Who We Are
Real Estate Intelligence
I needed to sell my house. Not because I wanted to. Because I had to. And the system that was supposed to help me — the MLS, the agent collecting 80%, the Zillow algorithm treating my home like a SKU — wasn't working. Every day it sat unsold, it got a little more damaged. That's how the market works. Time is a wound.

So I did what any English major who dropped out of film school would do. I built something. Not an app. Not a platform. A neighborhood — seen from above, in three dimensions, the way God sees it and Google wants to.

I've been doing this a long time. In White Center, Seattle — one of America's most forgotten neighborhoods — I once brought fruit to a food desert. Just showed up with produce where there was none. Nobody paid me. That's not the point. The point is that neighborhoods are alive. They breathe. They need tending.

Requation is that — for real estate. Not to make money. Not yet. First, to sell my own properties. Then to help every owner who's tired of waiting for a system that doesn't see them.
